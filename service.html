Service

Bryan College Station Middle School Robotics

I served as a volunteer for the local Bryan County middle schools as a judge for the regional FIRST Lego League (FLL) robotics competition. I helped set up, facilitate, score, and break down the event. This event was particularly fun because I was not only able to assist the students when they needed help, but I was also able to ask about each team's design and give insight on robotics hardware-software interfacing ensuring each team was evaluated correctly.

WE Code Volunteer Service

I founded the WE Code volunteer service to serve as a branch of WE in efforts to teach all ages how to program in a public setting. I organized the service to be located in the McKinney John and Judy Gay community library as an all-day event sponsored by WE. An introductory level programming curriculum was introduced to each student where a team of volunteers and I were able to teach fundamental concepts of programming in hopes of inspiring others to pursue the field of computer science.

Learn more about WE here.

Dowell Middle School TARC Volunteering

I volunteered for thee years as a mentor for Dowell Middle School Team America Rocketry Challenge (TARC) competition. I had the opportunity to teach students how to effectively design, simulate, model, launch, and retrieve model rockets with a wide set of constraints cast over weight, altitude, required payload, and rocket shape. For the time I served as a mentor I genuinely enjoyed teaching students how to collaboratively work on large projects together. I also had a lot of fun amplifying the students' interest in learning something new in hopes of it blossoming into a passion.
