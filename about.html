Howdy!

My name’s Albin Kyle Myscich IV and I’m from the Dallas Metroplex. I’m currently pursuing a double major in computer science and electrical engineering with a double minor in mathematics and cybersecurity at Texas A&M University. I’m actively researching robotics spatial motion planning in the A&M Distributed Artificial Intelligence Robotics Lab where I’m responsible for the hardware and software interfacing among intelligent systems design with ROS. Additionally, I partake in extracurricular activities as a general member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) and the Aggie Coding Club where I can maintain an edge on practical applications of software and industry standards.

Beyond the scope of school and work, I enjoy tinkering with 3D printer software slicers, building high power rockets, and Motocross ATV racing. But typically, you’ll find me programming, designing 3D models, Widlar-izing electronics, and running.

After I graduate, I hope to continue researching artificial intelligence and computer hardware-software interfacing. When I was younger, I was indirectly introduced to electronics when I was electrocuted plugging an adapter into a socket and later learning why it happened. Then soon after, inspired by a mutual friend who taught me how to build projects with RFID/NFC technology. While on the other hand, I was acquainted with artificial intelligence when Boston Dynamics first showcased BigDog’s ample reflexes in motion planning. Since then, I was inspired to design and build CPUs, pathway planning algorithms and motion tracking software used in iRobot models. Since I’ve been in college, both majors have crystalized my overarching interests in the world of technology and my optimism for better opportunities to follow. Although I don’t have a specific company I’d like to pursue at the moment, I hope to work in a collaborative environment that aligns with working towards building a future we can look forward to.
